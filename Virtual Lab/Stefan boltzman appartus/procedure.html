Test Procedure for Determining the Stefan Boltzmann Constant of Radiation Heat Transfer

AIM:To determine Stefan- Boltzman constant of radiation heat transfer.

APPARATUS:
Stefan- Boltzman Apparatus.

THEORY:

Stefan Boltzmann law is a basic law of radiation heat transfer and it states that at thermal equilibrium, the heat flux or emissive power of a black surface is proportional to the fourth power of absolute temperature of the surface and is given by: Q / A= εb σ T^4 (W/m^2)
The constant of proportionality σ is called the Stefan Boltzmann constant and its standard value is
5.667 x 10^(-8) W/(m^2 K^4).
The Stefan Boltzman law can be derived by integrating the Planck's law over the entire spectrum of wave lengths. Though historically it is worth noting that the Stefan Boltzmann law was independently developed before Planck's law.
